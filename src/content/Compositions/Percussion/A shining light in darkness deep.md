**A shining light in darkness deep** (2020) is originally a quartet for piano, glockenspiel (+bass drum), vibraphone, and marimba. In 2023, I created an alternative mallet quartet version for two marimbas and two vibraphones.

The work is made up of the following sections:

**slowly**, _like the push and pull of the tides_
**shimmering**, _like light reflecting through shallow water_  
**smooth**, _like small ripples of waves over a bottomless sea_  
**deep**, _like the vastness of the ocean floor_  
**singing**, _like a wisp of wind over the surface of the sea_  
**soothing**, _like the consistency of crashing waves_

This work finds inspiration in the words of Tom Marshall:

>_A song I heard the ocean sing  
>A shining light in darkness deep  
>I prayed a prayer into the tide  
>And both they soothed me in my sleep_

It was composed for the UCF New Music Ensemble but never premiered due to the COVID-19 pandemic. It was later premiered with the mallet quartet instrumentation at the 2023 Victoria Bach Festival in Victoria, TX.

Duration is approximately 4:00.
